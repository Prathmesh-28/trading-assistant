# QUANT-BACKLOG — what Citadel-class firms have that this bot doesn't (573 items)

Every item carries honest **pros** (what it buys YOU) and **cons** (cost/complexity/regulation/pointlessness at personal scale). Generated by a 10-agent audit. Companion to BACKLOG.md (product) / ROADMAP.md / STRATEGIES.md.

## Market access & latency (58)

1. **Exchange colocation racks at NSE Mahape/BKC** — Renting rack space inside NSE's colocation facility puts servers meters from the matching engine, cutting round-trip latency to microseconds; every Indian HFT (AlphaGrep, Tower's India arm, Quadeye) runs strategy servers there.
   - ✅ Would drop your order latency from hundreds of milliseconds (Render US free tier to Groww to NSE) to single-digit microseconds.
   - ⚠️ Costs Rs 15-30 lakh/year per rack, requires NSE trading membership, and is pointless for a signal engine where a human takes minutes to click a button.
2. **NSE trading membership (self-clearing)** — Direct exchange membership lets a firm route its own orders and clear trades without a retail broker; Citadel Securities and Jane Street hold memberships on every major exchange they trade.
   - ✅ Eliminates Groww's API rate limits, per-order fees, and dependence on a retail broker's uptime.
   - ⚠️ Requires Rs 1-5 crore in deposits and net-worth, SEBI registration, compliance officers, and audits — absurd overhead for one person's manual trades.
3. **Direct Market Access (DMA) order routing** — DMA sends orders straight to the exchange gateway under a sponsoring broker's ID, bypassing the broker's OMS; institutions use it for latency and control over order flags.
   - ✅ Removes Groww's app-layer latency and gives you native order types (IOC variants, disclosed qty) Groww hides.
   - ⚠️ In India DMA is restricted to institutional clients; retail cannot legally get it, and your one-tap manual flow gains nothing.
4. **Sponsored access with pre-trade risk checks** — Naked/sponsored access lets a firm's own servers hit the exchange using a member's ID with only thin exchange-mandated risk checks; Tower-style props use it to shave the broker hop.
   - ✅ Sub-100-microsecond order path instead of a REST call to Groww.
   - ⚠️ SEBI effectively bans naked access; sponsored setups need a member sponsor, legal agreements, and capital you don't have a use for.
5. **FPGA tick-to-trade pipeline** — Field-programmable gate arrays parse market data and emit orders entirely in hardware, achieving sub-microsecond tick-to-trade; Citadel Securities and Jump run FPGA feed handlers and order encoders.
   - ✅ Nanosecond-class reaction if you ever competed on speed.
   - ⚠️ Rs 50L+ in hardware and FPGA engineers, months of Verilog per protocol change, and your ORB strategy triggers on 5-minute bars — a 10^9x speed mismatch.
6. **Kernel-bypass networking (Solarflare/Onload, DPDK)** — User-space TCP/UDP stacks skip the Linux kernel, cutting tens of microseconds per packet; every HFT desk runs Onload or DPDK on market-data and order paths.
   - ✅ Faster WebSocket/quote handling on your server.
   - ⚠️ Needs dedicated NICs and bare metal (impossible on Render free tier), and saves microseconds in a flow with human-minutes decision latency.
7. **Exchange-native binary protocol handlers (NSE NNF/TAP)** — Firms implement NSE's native order-entry (NNF) and market-data binary protocols directly instead of vendor APIs, controlling every byte on the wire.
   - ✅ Full order-type coverage and no third-party API deprecations breaking your adapter.
   - ⚠️ Protocol specs are only released to members, need conformance testing with NSE, and Groww's REST API already covers a manual trader's needs.
8. **Multicast market data feed subscription (NSE TBT)** — NSE's tick-by-tick multicast feed delivers every order-book event, not conflated snapshots; market makers reconstruct full L3 books from it.
   - ✅ True order-by-order data instead of Groww's throttled LTP quotes, enabling real microstructure signals.
   - ⚠️ Only deliverable inside colo, costs lakhs annually, and produces terabytes/day your SQLite-and-lists architecture cannot ingest.
9. **Full depth-of-book (L2/L3) reconstruction engines** — In-house order-book builders maintain nanosecond-accurate replicas of the exchange book across all symbols; Jane Street's book builders feed every pricing model.
   - ✅ Would let your engine see spread, imbalance, and hidden liquidity before placing ideas.
   - ⚠️ Requires the TBT feed and a C++ team; your positional strategies work on daily candles and gain almost nothing from L3.
10. **Microwave/millimeter-wave links between venues** — Line-of-sight radio networks beat fiber by 30-40% on latency between exchange pairs; HFTs run Chicago-NJ and Mumbai-GIFT City microwave routes for cross-venue arbitrage.
   - ✅ None realistically — you trade one venue.
   - ⚠️ Tens of crores to build, licensed spectrum, and only matters for cross-venue arb strategies you don't run.
11. **Hollow-core fiber and shortest-path dark fiber** — Firms lease bespoke dark-fiber routes and hollow-core cable (light travels ~50% faster than in glass) between data centers to shave microseconds.
   - ✅ Nothing for a single-venue, human-speed workflow.
   - ⚠️ Millions in leases for microsecond gains that are invisible next to your multi-second Telegram notification path.
12. **PTP (IEEE 1588) hardware clock synchronization** — GPS-disciplined grandmaster clocks plus PTP-capable NICs sync all servers to sub-microsecond accuracy for event ordering and regulatory timestamping; mandatory at MiFID II firms.
   - ✅ Would make your backtest-vs-live timestamp comparisons trustworthy to the microsecond.
   - ⚠️ Needs dedicated hardware and colo; NTP on a cloud VM is off by milliseconds and that's fine when your bars are 5 minutes wide.
13. **Hardware timestamping NICs on every packet** — NICs that stamp packets at the PHY layer let firms measure true wire-to-wire latency and detect exchange gateway slowdowns; Tower instruments every hop.
   - ✅ You'd know exactly how slow the Groww API is at any moment.
   - ⚠️ Cloud hosting exposes no NIC hardware, and a simple software latency log gives you 95% of the diagnostic value.
14. **Tick-to-trade latency budgeting and per-hop attribution** — Institutions decompose total latency into feed-handler, strategy, risk-check, and encoder nanosecond budgets, with regression alerts on each hop; AlphaGrep tracks this per strategy per day.

## Market data infrastructure (58)

59. **NSE Tick-by-Tick (TBT) full order feed subscription** — NSE's TBT multicast feed delivers every order add/modify/cancel/trade for equities and F&O; HFTs like Tower and AlphaGrep consume it to reconstruct the full limit order book in microseconds.
   - ✅ True book visibility would let strategies see real supply/demand instead of 1-minute Groww candles.
   - ⚠️ Requires NSE membership or authorized vendor, colocation, and lakhs/month in fees — absurd for a bot placing manual trades via Telegram.
60. **Colocation rack in NSE Kohinoor/BKC data center** — Firms rent racks inside NSE's colo facility to receive multicast feeds and send orders with sub-100µs round trips.
   - ✅ Would eliminate all data latency for intraday ORB entries.
   - ⚠️ ₹15-40 lakh/year plus membership; meaningless when a human takes 30 seconds to tap an order.
61. **Hardware-timestamped packet capture (Solarflare/Napatech NICs)** — Capture cards stamp every inbound packet at the wire with nanosecond PTP-synced clocks; Citadel uses this for latency attribution and feed arbitration.
   - ✅ Ground-truth timestamps for slippage and fill research.
   - ⚠️ Thousands of dollars of hardware to timestamp data your broker already delays by seconds.
62. **A/B multicast feed arbitration and gap recovery** — Exchanges send duplicate A/B streams; feed handlers take the first-arriving packet per sequence number and issue retransmission requests on gaps (NSE's TBT recovery protocol).
   - ✅ Zero silent data holes versus your current best-effort REST/WS polling.
   - ⚠️ Only exists at raw-multicast level; Groww's API gives you no sequence numbers to arbitrate.
63. **In-house feed handlers per protocol (TBT, ITCH, MDP3)** — Custom C++/FPGA decoders for each exchange's binary protocol (NASDAQ ITCH, CME MDP3, NSE TBT) normalizing to an internal message bus; Jane Street writes these in OCaml.
   - ✅ Full control over decode latency and field fidelity.
   - ⚠️ Person-years of engineering to replace one `requests.get` call.
64. **FPGA-based feed decode and book build** — Tower/AlphaGrep decode exchange packets and maintain top-of-book on FPGAs, cutting decode latency from microseconds to ~100ns.
   - ✅ None realistically — your alpha horizon is hours to weeks.
   - ⚠️ Verilog engineers and $10k+ boards for a strategy that trades off daily candles.
65. **Full-depth limit order book reconstruction engine** — Software that replays add/modify/delete messages into per-symbol price-level and order-level books, the substrate for queue-position and imbalance signals.

   - ⚠️ Needs TBT feed access first; without it this is a book built on nothing.

   - ✅ Perfect-fidelity backtests instead of 1-minute-candle approximations.
   - ⚠️ Petabytes of S3/tape and a data-eng team; your 730-day candle history fits in SQLite.
69. **Columnar tick database (kdb+/q, ClickHouse, Arrow/Parquet lake)** — kdb+ is the industry standard for as-of joins and nanosecond time-series queries over trillions of ticks.

## Execution algorithms & order routing (57)

117. **VWAP execution algorithm** — Slices a parent order along the historical intraday volume curve so fills track the day's volume-weighted average price; every sell-side and buy-side desk uses it as the default benchmark algo.
   - ✅ Would let the user work a larger positional entry through the day instead of one market order at the open.
   - ⚠️ At 1-10 lakh order sizes on NIFTY50 names your market impact is near zero, so VWAP slicing mostly adds latency and code to maintain.
118. **TWAP execution algorithm** — Slices an order into equal time buckets regardless of volume, used by firms for illiquid names or when hiding intent from volume-profile-aware predators.
   - ✅ Simple to implement on top of the existing Groww adapter and reduces timing luck on entries.
   - ⚠️ Predictable clip cadence is trivially gameable and pointless when a single retail order clears the book without moving it.
119. **POV / participation-rate algo** — Targets a fixed percentage of live traded volume (e.g. 10% POV), throttling child orders in real time; core algo at Citadel-style desks for liquidity-sensitive orders.
   - ✅ Would cap footprint if the user ever trades small/midcap NSE names with thin books.
   - ⚠️ Requires real-time consolidated volume feed and continuous order management the Render free tier and Groww rate limits cannot sustain.
120. **Implementation Shortfall (Almgren-Chriss) algo** — Optimizes the impact-vs-timing-risk tradeoff against the arrival price using a market-impact model; the canonical institutional cost-minimizing algo.
   - ✅ Formalizes the 'how fast should I get in' question the engine currently ignores.
   - ⚠️ Calibrating impact parameters needs millions of own-fill observations the user will never generate.
121. **Adaptive / opportunistic liquidity-seeking algo** — Dynamically shifts between passive and aggressive child orders based on spread, queue depth, and short-term alpha signals (e.g. Citadel's tactical algos).
   - ✅ Could shave a few bps per fill on wide-spread names.
   - ⚠️ Needs tick-level L2 data and sub-second decision loops — orders of magnitude beyond a polling FastAPI bot.
122. **Close/MOC-benchmarked algo** — Targets the closing auction price by scheduling participation into the NSE closing session, standard for index-tracking and EOD-benchmarked flow.
   - ✅ 12-1 momentum rotation rebalances are naturally close-benchmarked, so this matches the strategy's own benchmark.
   - ⚠️ NSE closing auction mechanics differ from US MOC and the gain over 'place order at 15:20' is a bp or two.
123. **Smart order router (SOR)** — Routes each child order to the venue with the best price/liquidity across exchanges; firms route across NSE/BSE and dozens of US venues with venue-cost models.
   - ✅ NSE vs BSE arbitrage on dual-listed names occasionally gives a tick of price improvement.
   - ⚠️ Groww's retail API doesn't expose venue choice per order in a useful way, and SEBI's best-execution landscape makes the edge tiny.
124. **Venue toxicity scoring** — Scores each venue/counterparty pool by post-fill adverse selection (markouts) and steers flow away from toxic ones; a core Tower/Citadel Securities routing input.
   - ✅ None materially — user has effectively one venue.
   - ⚠️ Meaningless with a single broker on a single exchange; pure overkill.
125. **Iceberg / disclosed-quantity orders** — Shows only a small visible tranche of a large resting order and reloads as it fills; used to hide size (NSE natively supports disclosed quantity).
   - ✅ Actually cheap to add — NSE disclosed-quantity is a one-field change in the order payload if Groww exposes it.
   - ⚠️ Hiding size only matters when your size can move the book, which the user's cannot.
126. **Pegged order types** — Orders that automatically float with the bid/mid/offer (primary peg, midpoint peg), used to maintain passive queue presence without re-quoting.
   - ✅ Would keep limit entries near the touch without the bot repricing.
   - ⚠️ NSE doesn't offer native pegs to retail; emulating them means constant cancel/replace against broker rate limits.
127. **IOC / FOK order-type usage** — Immediate-or-cancel and fill-or-kill orders for taking displayed liquidity without leaving resting exposure, the workhorse of aggressive institutional taking.
   - ✅ IOC is available on NSE via most broker APIs and would eliminate stale resting orders after signal invalidation.
   - ⚠️ Marginal benefit when the bot already places at-market fills the human confirms; FOK is near-useless at retail size.
128. **Child-order scheduling engine** — A scheduler that plans parent-to-child decomposition (size, timing, aggression) and replans on fills/misses; the spine of any institutional algo stack.
   - ✅ Gives a clean abstraction if the user ever adds any slicing at all.
   - ⚠️ Significant stateful engineering (order state machine, partial fills, amend races) for a bot whose orders currently fill in one shot.

## Portfolio construction & risk math (58)

344. **Barra-style multi-factor equity risk model** — Cross-sectional factor model (style, industry, country factors) decomposing each stock's return/risk into common factors plus specific risk; Citadel and every long/short shop uses vendor (MSCI Barra, Axioma) or in-house versions to measure and hedge unintended exposures.
   - ✅ Would show whether your 'momentum rotation' portfolio is actually a leveraged bet on one industry or beta rather than the signal.
   - ⚠️ Building estimation universes and factor returns for NSE requires clean fundamental data and hundreds of names; vendor licenses run six figures — massive overkill for a 10-position personal book.
345. **Daily factor exposure attribution report** — Nightly decomposition of PnL into factor contributions (momentum, value, size, beta, industry) vs idiosyncratic alpha, used at firms to detect style drift and reward true alpha.
   - ✅ Tells you if your strategies earn real edge or just harvest beta you could buy with an index fund.
   - ⚠️ Needs the factor model above first; a poor-man's regression against NIFTY sector indices gets 80% of the value in 50 lines.
346. **Statistical (PCA) risk model as factor-model fallback** — Principal-component decomposition of the return covariance matrix to extract latent risk factors without fundamental data; Tower/AlphaGrep-style stat-arb desks use these for fast-moving risk.
   - ✅ Buildable from your existing candle history alone; catches correlated blowup risk across your positions.
   - ⚠️ Components are unlabeled and unstable across re-estimation, so interpreting 'PC2 exposure' is guesswork at n=50 symbols.
347. **Ledoit-Wolf covariance shrinkage estimator** — Shrinks the noisy sample covariance matrix toward a structured target (constant-correlation or identity) with an analytically optimal intensity; standard preprocessing before any institutional optimizer.
   - ✅ A 30-line sklearn-free implementation that makes any correlation-aware sizing you add actually stable with only ~250 daily bars.
   - ⚠️ Only matters once you size by covariance at all; with 3-8 concurrent positions the sample matrix barely exists to shrink.
348. **Nonlinear/eigenvalue-clipping covariance cleaning (RMT)** — Random-matrix-theory denoising (Marchenko-Pastur clipping, Ledoit-Wolf nonlinear shrinkage) of correlation eigenvalues, used by quant funds when N assets approaches T observations.
   - ✅ Marginally better correlation estimates for your NIFTY50 universe stats page.
   - ⚠️ Academic-grade complexity for a personal book; plain shrinkage is indistinguishable at your scale.
349. **EWMA / RiskMetrics-style dynamic covariance** — Exponentially weighted covariance (lambda ~0.94) so risk estimates react to regime shifts within days, the JP Morgan RiskMetrics standard every risk desk starts from.
   - ✅ Your portfolio heat caps would tighten automatically when NSE volatility spikes instead of using stale trailing vol.
   - ⚠️ Adds a tunable decay parameter and more whipsaw in position sizes; your ATR-based stops already capture much of this.
350. **DCC-GARCH or multivariate GARCH correlation forecasting** — Engle's dynamic conditional correlation model forecasting how correlations rise in stress, used by risk teams to anticipate diversification failure.
   - ✅ Would warn that your five 'diversified' NIFTY positions become one trade in a crash.
   - ⚠️ Fiddly MLE estimation, fragile convergence, and EWMA correlation gets you 90% of the effect for 5% of the code.
351. **Mean-variance (Markowitz) portfolio optimizer with constraints** — Quadratic-programming allocation maximizing expected return minus risk-aversion-times-variance under position/sector/turnover constraints; the baseline sizing engine at every systematic fund.
   - ✅ Replaces your fixed per-trade sizing with allocations that account for cross-position correlation.
   - ⚠️ Notoriously an 'error maximizer' — garbage-in expected returns produce extreme corner solutions; needs shrinkage plus constraints to be usable, and with <10 positions equal-risk sizing is nearly as good.
352. **Black-Litterman expected-return blending** — Bayesian framework blending market-implied equilibrium returns with discretionary 'views' at chosen confidence, used by asset allocators to tame Markowitz instability.
   - ✅ A principled way to convert your strategy signals ('RELIANCE bullish, 60% confidence') into portfolio weights.
   - ⚠️ Equilibrium prior needs market-cap weights and a risk-aversion calibration that is essentially made up at personal scale; lots of machinery for modest sizing tweaks.
353. **Hierarchical Risk Parity (HRP) allocation** — Lopez de Prado's tree-clustering allocator that avoids inverting the covariance matrix, popular at quant funds for robustness with short histories.
   - ✅ Drop-in, optimizer-free diversification across your concurrent positions using only the correlation matrix — genuinely feasible for this bot.
   - ⚠️ Ignores expected returns entirely, so it will happily allocate to your weakest signal; clustering on 5 assets is mostly ceremony.
354. **Risk-parity / equal-risk-contribution sizing** — Sizes each position so it contributes equal marginal volatility to the portfolio, the Bridgewater-style baseline used across multi-strategy books.
   - ✅ Cheap upgrade to your heat model: a high-beta smallcap idea automatically gets a smaller notional than an FMCG name.
   - ⚠️ Vol targeting systematically overweights low-vol names that may be low-vol precisely because they're dead money.
355. **Full Kelly / fractional-Kelly position sizing** — Sizes bets proportional to edge-over-variance from estimated win rate and payoff; prop shops and Jane Street-style desks use fractional Kelly (quarter to half) as the growth-optimal ceiling.
   - ✅ Uses your backtester's per-strategy win/loss stats to size trades by demonstrated edge instead of a flat rupee amount.
   - ⚠️ Kelly is brutally sensitive to edge estimation error — a backtest-overfit win rate produces oversized bets exactly when the edge is fake; must run at <=25% Kelly to be safe.
356. **Bayesian edge estimation with shrinkage across strategies** — Hierarchical priors that shrink each strategy's estimated win rate toward the cross-strategy mean until sample size justifies otherwise, used to keep Kelly-style sizing honest on young strategies.
   - ✅ Stops the bot from oversizing a strategy that went 8-for-10 on ten trades.
   - ⚠️ Requires committing to priors and a bit of PyMC/Stan or hand-rolled beta-binomial math; simpler trade-count minimums achieve similar protection.
357. **Parametric and historical Value-at-Risk (VaR)** — Daily 95/99% loss quantile of the current portfolio via variance-covariance or historical simulation, the universal risk-desk headline number and regulatory standard.
   - ✅ One number on your dashboard — 'a bad day costs about Rs X' — far more meaningful than gross heat.
   - ⚠️ VaR says nothing about tail size beyond the quantile and famously fails in crises; easy to compute, easy to false-comfort.
358. **Expected Shortfall (CVaR / ES) with CVaR-constrained sizing** — Average loss beyond the VaR quantile, now the Basel FRTB standard; firms optimize portfolios subject to ES limits via Rockafellar-Uryasev linear programming.
   - ✅ Captures the fat left tail of Indian smallcap circuits better than VaR and is coherent (subadditive).
   - ⚠️ Tail estimates on ~250 daily observations are dominated by 3-5 datapoints; the LP optimization layer is overkill versus a simple ES readout.
359. **Monte Carlo VaR with fat-tailed / t-copula return simulation** — Simulating portfolio paths from Student-t marginals joined by a t-copula to capture joint tail dependence, standard at derivatives-heavy desks like Citadel.
   - ✅ You already have Monte Carlo in the backtester — extending it to joint fat-tailed portfolio risk is a natural step.
   - ⚠️ Copula parameter estimation on Indian equities with your data depth is largely fiction dressed as precision.
360. **Extreme Value Theory (EVT) tail modeling** — Generalized-Pareto fitting of the loss tail beyond a threshold to estimate 99.9% quantiles, used by risk teams for capital adequacy beyond observable history.
   - ✅ Honest estimate of the once-a-decade loss for your positional book.
   - ⚠️ Threshold choice is arbitrary, estimates have huge confidence intervals, and the actionable output ('bad tails are bad') rarely changes a personal trader's behavior.
361. **Incremental and component VaR per position** — Marginal contribution of each holding to total portfolio VaR (Euler decomposition), used by PMs to identify which position to trim first under a risk limit.
   - ✅ Turns 'reduce risk' into 'trim TATASTEEL specifically'; directly slots into your exit-review loop.
   - ⚠️ Requires a covariance estimate first; with few positions eyeballing gets close.
362. **Historical scenario stress-test library** — Replaying named crises (2008 GFC, 2013 taper tantrum, 2016 demonetization, COVID Mar-2020, 2024 election-day gap) against the current portfolio, a mandated exercise at every regulated fund.
   - ✅ Concrete answer to 'what does my current book lose on an election-shock day' — highly buildable from your existing candle store.
   - ⚠️ Past crises never repeat exactly; scenario selection bias gives false completeness.
363. **Hypothetical / factor-shock stress testing** — Synthetic shocks (NIFTY -8%, INR -3%, crude +15%, banking sector -12%) propagated through factor betas to portfolio PnL, used by risk committees for forward-looking what-ifs.
   - ✅ Lets you test the book against Indian-specific macro risks (monsoon, budget day, RBI surprise) history hasn't shown yet.
   - ⚠️ Only as good as your beta estimates; without a factor model the propagation is a single index beta, i.e., a multiplication.
364. **Reverse stress testing** — Solving for the scenario that produces a specified unacceptable loss (e.g., -20% of equity), a regulatory requirement for banks and a Citadel risk-committee staple.
   - ✅ Surfaces the specific correlated failure (gap-through-stops on 3 positions simultaneously) your heat caps silently assume away.
   - ⚠️ An optimization over a scenario space you must define; at personal scale a thought experiment on paper delivers the same insight.
365. **Gap-risk / overnight-jump modeling in position sizing** — Explicitly modeling the probability and size of open-gaps through stop levels (jump-diffusion or empirical gap distributions), critical on NSE where circuits and gaps make stops non-binding; every intraday firm sizes to gap risk not stop distance.
   - ✅ Directly fixes the biggest lie in your current risk math — that a stop at -2% caps the loss at -2%.
   - ⚠️ Empirical gap distributions per symbol need years of data and still can't price a one-off event like a fraud disclosure.
366. **Circuit-limit and trading-halt risk modeling** — Modeling NSE price bands (5/10/20% circuits) and halt probability as liquidity risk — positions can be untradeable for days — something India-focused firms like AlphaGrep bake into sizing.
   - ✅ Prevents oversizing in band-limited smallcaps where your engine's stop is pure fiction.
   - ⚠️ Mostly relevant outside NIFTY50; for your current universe a per-symbol liquidity flag suffices.
367. **Formal drawdown control policy (equity curve governor)** — Predefined rulebook that cuts gross exposure at drawdown thresholds (e.g., -5% halve risk, -10% flat, cooldown before re-risking), a written policy at every multi-strat pod including Citadel's famous pod stop-outs.
   - ✅ Cheapest, highest-value item on this list: a codified 'when losing, trade smaller' rule enforced by the engine, not willpower.
   - ⚠️ Guarantees you're smallest exactly when a mean-reverting strategy is about to recover; threshold choice is a real strategy decision.
368. **Volatility targeting at the portfolio level** — Scaling total gross exposure to hit a constant annualized portfolio vol (e.g., 12%), the standard risk chassis at AQR/Man and most CTAs.
   - ✅ Smooths your equity curve and makes performance comparable across regimes; simple to bolt onto your heat cap.
   - ⚠️ Mechanically sells after vol spikes (sell-low behavior) and adds turnover costs your costs.py will faithfully report.
369. **Conditional Drawdown-at-Risk (CDaR) optimization** — Portfolio optimization minimizing expected drawdown depth rather than variance (Chekhlov-Uryasev), used by drawdown-sensitive allocators and prop capital.
   - ✅ Optimizes the metric a personal trader actually feels — drawdown — not variance.
   - ⚠️ An LP over path-dependent quantities needing long return paths per strategy; with your trade counts the estimates are noise.
370. **Capital allocation across strategies via risk budgeting** — Explicit risk budget per strategy (ORB gets 30% of portfolio vol, positional 50%, rotation 20%) rebalanced on realized performance, the core mechanism of a Citadel-style multi-strategy allocator.
   - ✅ Right now your strategies implicitly compete for capital first-come-first-served; a budget makes the tradeoff deliberate and measurable.
   - ⚠️ Reallocating on realized Sharpe with <100 trades per strategy is chasing noise; budgets should move slowly or not at all.
371. **Strategy-level Sharpe/Deflated-Sharpe monitoring with kill thresholds** — Bailey-Lopez de Prado deflated Sharpe ratio and probabilistic Sharpe tests that decide when live performance statistically contradicts the backtest, triggering strategy retirement — the multi-strat pod stop-out formalized.
   - ✅ An objective 'this strategy is dead' rule instead of hoping ORB comes back.
   - ⚠️ Statistical power is terrible at personal trade frequency — you may need two years of live trades to reject a broken strategy.
372. **Alpha decay tracking per signal** — Measuring how a strategy's information coefficient / per-trade edge trends over time as the anomaly gets arbitraged, standard signal-lifecycle hygiene at stat-arb firms.
   - ✅ Early warning that gap-and-go edge is eroding before the equity curve makes it obvious.
   - ⚠️ Requires many trades per estimation window; on 5 signals a month the decay curve is indistinguishable from variance.
373. **Correlation-aware concurrent-position limits** — Heat caps expressed in correlation-adjusted risk units (effective number of independent bets) rather than raw position count, how every risk desk defines concentration.
   - ✅ Stops the engine approving five bank stocks that are one trade; a genuine upgrade to your existing heat cap with modest code.
   - ⚠️ Correlation estimates lag regime changes, so the cap loosens exactly when correlations are about to converge to 1.
374. **Sector / industry / single-name concentration limits** — Hard caps on exposure per GICS sector and per issuer (e.g., max 20% sector, 8% name), a boilerplate mandate constraint at every institution.
   - ✅ Trivial to add to handle_command approval flow and prevents the most common personal-account blowup (all-in on one theme).
   - ⚠️ Arbitrary thresholds; with a small account, caps can block the only good ideas the engine has that week.

## Risk controls & compliance (57)

   - ✅ Guarantees a runaway loop can never emit an order violating limits, independent of Python bugs.

   - ✅ A single config typo (qty 1000 vs 100) gets blocked instead of executed; cheap insurance for one-tap Groww execution.
   - ⚠️ Almost none — this is one of the few items genuinely worth building; only cost is choosing sane thresholds and handling false rejects.
404. **Price-band and LULD/circuit-limit validation** — Pre-trade check that limit prices sit within exchange price bands (NSE 2/5/10/20% bands, US LULD), so orders are never rejected or filled at absurd prices; every institutional OMS does this against daily band files.

   - ⚠️ Requires ingesting NSE daily price-band files and handling band revisions intraday — real maintenance for rare saves.

   - ✅ Caps worst-case fill on illiquid NSE midcaps where a market order can slip several percent.

## Fundamentals & screener-style data (57)

517. **Point-in-time fundamentals database** — Stores financials as they were known on each date (original filings, pre-restatement) so backtests see only information available then; every quant fund uses PIT data (Compustat PIT, FactSet) to avoid restatement lookahead bias.
   - ✅ Would make any fundamental-factor backtest on your NIFTY universe honest instead of contaminated by restated numbers.
   - ⚠️ PIT vendors (FactSet, S&P) cost tens of thousands USD/year; building your own requires archiving every BSE/NSE filing from today forward — years before it's useful.
518. **Standardized financial statement history (10+ years)** — Normalized quarterly/annual P&L, balance sheet, cash flow across all listed companies mapped to a common template; firms use it as the substrate for every fundamental factor.
   - ✅ Enables ROE/ROCE/margin trend screens across NIFTY500 instead of hand-checking screener.in per stock.
   - ⚠️ Indian free sources (yfinance, NSE) give shallow history and inconsistent line items; paid vendors (Ace Equity, CMIE Prowess, Refinitiv) run lakhs/year; scraping screener.in violates ToS.
519. **Consolidated vs standalone statement handling** — Logic choosing consolidated or standalone financials per company (holding companies, banks) consistently; fundamental funds hard-code entity-level rules to avoid double counting subsidiaries.
   - ✅ Prevents garbage ratios on conglomerates like Reliance or Tata Motors where standalone numbers mislead.
   - ⚠️ Requires per-company judgment calls and maintenance; irrelevant until you actually compute fundamental factors at all.
520. **Automated XBRL filing ingestion from exchanges** — Parser pulling structured XBRL financials directly from NSE/BSE/MCA filings the hour they drop; HFT-adjacent fundamental desks parse filings machine-speed for event trades.
   - ✅ Legal, free primary-source data replacing screener.in scraping — exchange filings are public.
   - ⚠️ Indian XBRL taxonomies are messy and change; MCA access is paywalled per-document; substantial parser engineering for data you'd read quarterly anyway.
521. **Earnings estimates and consensus (IBES-style)** — Analyst EPS/revenue estimates with revision history; funds trade estimate-revision momentum and earnings-surprise (SUE) factors off it.
   - ✅ Estimate-revision momentum is one of the most robust factors and would complement your 12-1 price momentum.
   - ⚠️ Refinitiv IBES / Bloomberg cost is institutional-only; no free Indian consensus source with revision history exists; Trendlyne partial data has no API.
522. **Earnings surprise (SUE) factor engine** — Standardized unexpected earnings computed vs consensus or time-series model, used for post-earnings-announcement-drift strategies at virtually every quant shop.
   - ✅ PEAD persists in India and fits your positional 1-4 week holding horizon perfectly.
   - ⚠️ Needs the consensus feed above plus precise announcement timestamps; without both it degrades to naive YoY-growth screens.
523. **Earnings announcement date calendar with timestamps** — Forward calendar of board-meeting/results dates and exact release times; firms use it to gate risk (no positions through prints) and to time event strategies.
   - ✅ Your engine could auto-flag 'earnings in 2 days' on open positions — a real risk gap today.
   - ⚠️ NSE publishes board-meeting dates free, so the ingestion is easy; the con is only that dates slip and intraday release timing still needs manual verification.
524. **Corporate actions master (splits, bonuses, buybacks, demergers)** — Golden-source corporate-action feed adjusting prices/shares and generating event signals; every fund's PnL and factor engine depends on it being perfect.
   - ✅ Protects your momentum ranks and stops from fake -50% moves on split days.
   - ⚠️ Groww/yfinance adjust prices already for splits; full coverage (demergers, rights) needs a paid feed or careful NSE circular parsing.
525. **Dividend history and forecast dataset** — Ex-date/record-date/amount history plus forecast dividends; used for total-return calculation, dividend-capture strategies, and carry-adjusted futures pricing.
   - ✅ Fixes your backtester's silent understatement of returns on high-yield names like Coal India.
   - ⚠️ A few percent accuracy improvement for meaningful ingestion work; NSE data is free but fragmented across circulars.
526. **Shareholding pattern time series (promoter/FII/DII/retail)** — Quarterly SEBI-mandated shareholding breakdowns tracked as a time series; India-focused funds trade FII/DII flow tilts and promoter-stake-change signals.
   - ✅ Promoter buying and FII accumulation are genuinely informative in Indian small/midcaps you might trade.
   - ⚠️ Quarterly frequency with 21-day lag limits alpha; parsing BSE shareholding filings reliably is tedious but legal and free.

   - ✅ A hard 'pledge > 50% = no trade' filter would have saved retail traders from several -60% Indian blowups.

   - ✅ Insider cluster-buying is a documented positive signal in India and NSE publishes disclosures free.
